Soft brewing methods

Aside from the famous espresso, there are a lot of other techniques to create a memorable coffee cup. Soft brewing methods don’t require mechanical pressure to extract the flavor from the coffee beans. These methods can be divided into 2 categories: percolation and immersion.

With percolation brewing, water is usually poured over the ground coffee, which allows for the extraction as the water is flowing through the ground coffee beans. There are different ways of brewing a pour-over coffee: using a V60, a chemex, a Kalita Wave, a simple percolator…



One aspect to keep in mind with pour-over coffee is a phenomenon called channeling. Channeling happens when there are uniformities in the coffee bed, due to the size of the coffee particles and the quality of the grind. When coffee is poured over the coffee bed, the water might flow faster in some areas, creating channels where most of the water will flow. This will cause some parts of the coffee to be over-extracted, while other parts will be under-extracted, resulting in an uneven extraction profile and thus impacting the taste of your brew. Avoiding channeling is therefore a must, but it is not always easy.

Brewing by immersion involves submerging the coffee ground in water. Therefore, this technique usually requires filtration in order to seperate the brew from the coffee grounds. Examples of submersion brewing are the French press, siphon and Aeropress.




Step by step guide for brewing a coffee using a V60 dripper

This method is based on osmotic flow, therefore a slightly coarser grind should be used (1000μm)

This particular technique results in a softer coffee, with low acidity and some bitter notes. It gives more body and texture to the coffee cup. This technique is also suited for darker roasts.

Note that for this technique no agitation is needed.

Step 1

Clean the filter with hot water and install it correctly. Set the scale and timer to zero.

Step 2

Add the ground coffee (+/- (1000μm) and pour it in the filter. Equalise the coffee bed by slightly tapping the side of the filter holder.

Step 3

Pre-infusion: add 2-3g of water per gram of coffee. Then, wait for 40s.

Step 4:

SLOWLY pour 50% of the water volume, only in the center of the coffee grounds.

Step 5

Interrupt the water pouring everytime to coffee bed rises, and wait for the water the drip until you see the coffee bed again.

Step 6:

Then, slowly pour the left-over 50% of the water in spirals.

Step 7:

Interrupt the water pouring everytime to coffee bed rises, and wait for the water the drip until you see the coffee bed again.

Step 8:

Final pour: around the edges to get any leftover coffee stuck to the sides.
